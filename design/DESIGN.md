# DESIGN.md — Arvixy

## 1. Brand Overview

**Arvixy** is a technology company focused on:

- custom software development
- process automation
- technology consulting

The brand should feel modern, intelligent, premium, reliable, and efficient.

Arvixy does not sell generic websites or isolated code. It helps companies improve operations, reduce manual work, and scale with technology.

---

## 2. Brand Personality

The interface should communicate:

- clarity
- innovation
- precision
- trust
- business value
- technical solvency

Avoid looking overly corporate and cold.
Avoid looking playful or informal.
Avoid looking like a generic agency template.

The ideal perception is:

> A modern technology partner that understands business problems and turns them into practical digital solutions.

---

## 3. Visual Direction

### Recommended direction

Use a **premium hybrid technology style**:

- mostly light layout for trust and readability
- dark accent sections for impact and contrast
- modern tech details in gradients, glows, dashboards, and abstract flow visuals
- clean structure with premium spacing and strong typography

This direction combines the strengths of:

- clear premium-tech
- dark technological style

Result:

- more commercial credibility
- better readability
- more modern visual identity
- strong brand differentiation without losing seriousness

---

## 4. Target Audience

Design for:

- small and medium businesses
- operations-driven companies
- founders and business owners
- companies with manual processes to optimize
- teams that need custom internal tools or automation

The design should work for both:

- traditional decision-makers who need trust
- modern companies who value innovation

---

## 5. Core Message

The website should clearly communicate this idea:

> Arvixy develops software and automates processes to help businesses operate better, faster, and with more control.

Secondary ideas:

- technology aligned to business goals
- less manual work
- more efficiency
- practical and scalable solutions
- technical execution with strategic thinking

---

## 6. Design Principles

### 6.1 Clarity first

Every section must be immediately understandable.
Short headlines, concise supporting text, clear hierarchy.

### 6.2 Business before decoration

Visual style must support credibility and conversion.
Design should feel intentional, not ornamental.

### 6.3 Modern but controlled

Use advanced visual language, but avoid excess.
No visual chaos, no overloaded motion, no neon abuse.

### 6.4 Responsive by design

Design mobile-first.
The mobile experience must feel premium, not reduced.

### 6.5 Process-oriented visual storytelling

Whenever possible, use visuals that suggest:

- flows
- systems
- automation
- dashboards
- connected tools
- structured operations

---

## 7. Layout Strategy

### General layout

- strong hero section
- modular sections with generous spacing
- alternating light and dark blocks for rhythm
- cards for services and benefits
- simple but premium footer

### Content flow

1. Explain what Arvixy does
2. Show business value
3. Present services
4. Build trust with process and differentiators
5. End with clear contact CTA

---

## 8. Color System

### Base palette

**Background light:** `#F8FAFC`
**Surface:** `#FFFFFF`
**Dark section:** `#0F172A`
**Dark accent background:** `#111827`
**Primary blue:** `#2563EB`
**Cyan accent:** `#06B6D4`
**Violet accent:** `#7C3AED`
**Primary text:** `#0F172A`
**Secondary text:** `#475569`
**Border soft:** `#E2E8F0`

### Gradient suggestions

- blue → cyan
- blue → violet
- cyan → violet (only for accent moments)

### Usage rules

- main sections should stay highly readable
- use dark backgrounds in hero, CTA, or emphasis blocks
- use vibrant colors as accents, not as the entire interface base
- maintain strong contrast in text and buttons

---

## 9. Typography

### Recommended fonts

Primary options:

- Inter
- Manrope
- Space Grotesk

### Recommended use

- headlines: Space Grotesk or Manrope
- body: Inter
- UI labels/buttons: Inter or Manrope

### Typography style

- bold, compact headlines
- short paragraphs
- comfortable line-height
- visually strong section titles

Avoid long dense text blocks.

---

## 10. UI Components Style

### Buttons

- rounded but not exaggerated
- primary solid button in blue
- secondary outline or ghost button
- hover states with subtle lift or glow

### Cards

- soft radius
- subtle shadows on light backgrounds
- optional glass effect only on dark hero/feature areas
- clear icon + title + short description structure

### Navbar

- sticky header
- semi-transparent background on scroll
- clean links
- CTA button always visible

### Inputs / Form

- clean modern inputs
- generous padding
- subtle borders
- strong focus state in blue/cyan

---

## 11. Imagery and Graphics

Use visuals inspired by:

- dashboards
- connected systems
- workflow maps
- abstract automation flows
- data movement
- interface mockups

Preferred visual assets:

- software dashboard mockups
- abstract lines and node networks
- UI cards floating in space
- gradient glows behind illustrations

Avoid:

- cliché stock photos of people shaking hands
- generic office smiles
- robotic imagery that feels cheap
- overused AI/brain icons

---

## 12. Motion and Interaction

Use motion carefully.

Recommended:

- fade and slide on scroll
- subtle hover transitions
- light parallax in hero visuals
- floating visual layers with soft movement

Avoid:

- heavy animations
- distracting motion loops
- anything that hurts performance on mobile

Motion should suggest:

- flow
- speed
- smart systems
- digital continuity

---

## 13. Mobile Experience

Mobile is not secondary.

Requirements:

- hero text must remain short and powerful
- CTA visible early
- stacked cards with good spacing
- fast loading feeling
- readable font sizes
- simplified but elegant visual effects
- compact navbar with hamburger menu

Mobile should feel:

- clean
- fast
- premium
- easy to scan

---

4. Services
5. Benefits

7. Use cases

9. About Arvixy

---

## 15. Copy Tone

The copy should feel:

- direct
- clear
- business-oriented
- modern
- technically solid

Avoid:

- excessive buzzwords
- vague promises
- very long paragraphs
- language that sounds generic or inflated

Good tone example:

> We build software and automate processes so your business can operate with more efficiency, control, and scalability.

---

## 16. Conversion Goals

The homepage should drive users toward:

- scheduling a consultation
- sending a contact inquiry
- understanding Arvixy's value quickly

Every major section should support trust and movement toward contact.

Recommended CTA labels:

- Agendar consulta
- Solicitar una consulta
- Hablemos
- Contactanos

---

## 17. Brand Keywords

Use these as emotional anchors for the design:

- efficient
- intelligent
- modern
- scalable
- reliable
- clear
- premium
- technological

---

## 18. Final Creative Guidance

Design Arvixy as a premium technology partner for businesses.

The website should feel like:

- a serious software company
- a smart automation consultancy
- a modern digital operator

It should not feel like:

- a template agency
- a personal portfolio
- a trendy but empty startup site

Final goal:

> Create a responsive corporate website that looks modern and memorable, but above all makes Arvixy feel trustworthy, capable, and strategically valuable.
